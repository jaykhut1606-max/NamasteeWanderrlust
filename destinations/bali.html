Day-by-Day

Your 7 days in Bali

A pace that feels like a holiday, not a checklist. Mornings are early for Nusa Penida and Mt Batur; afternoons leave breathing room for pools, spas and slow dinners.

01

Day 1 · Arrival Day

Arrive Denpasar → Benoa Beach → Uluwatu Temple

Land at Ngurah Rai International Airport, clear Visa on Arrival, and meet your NamasteeWanderrlust host at the gate. Check into Adhi Jaya Kuta by noon, grab a quick nasi goreng, then head to Benoa Beach for a welcome dip. As the light softens, we drive to Uluwatu Temple for sunset and the famous fire-lit Kecak dance on the cliffside. Welcome dinner by the ocean.

Airport pickup Uluwatu Kecak dance Welcome dinner
02

Day 2 · Island Day

Nusa Penida day tour

The iconic Bali day. Early morning speedboat from Sanur to Nusa Penida, then a 4x4 jeep tour of the west coast: Kelingking Beach's T-Rex cliff viewpoint, Broken Beach's natural arch, Angel's Billabong's tidal pool, and Crystal Bay for snorkelling and lunch. Back to Sanur by sunset. Long day — but this is the reel everyone comes home with.

Kelingking Beach Broken Beach Angel's Billabong Crystal Bay
03

Day 3 · North & West Coast

Bedugul temple → Tanah Lot sunset

Drive north through mountain fog to Ulun Danu Beratan — Bali's lake temple that looks like it's floating. Stop at Jatiluwih rice fields for coffee (much quieter than Tegallalang). Late afternoon we swing back toward the coast for Tanah Lot, the sea temple perched on a rock. Stay for sunset with a coconut in hand, back at the hotel for dinner.

Ulun Danu Beratan Jatiluwih coffee stop Tanah Lot sunset
04

Day 4 · Transfer to Ubud

Tegallalang rice terraces & Bali Swing

Pack up, check out of Kuta, and head into Bali's green heart. First stop: Tegallalang, the famous tiered rice fields (we go early to beat the crowds). Then the Bali Swing for the cinematic jungle shot. Check into Sens Hotel Ubud in the afternoon — your host shoots a golden-hour reel round at the pool, then we walk into town for a slow Ubud dinner.

Tegallalang Bali Swing Sens Hotel Ubud check-in
05

Day 5 · Volcano Sunrise

Mt Batur jeep sunrise → Tirta Empul

3:30 AM wake-up (worth it, promise). Hop in a 4x4 jeep that drives you right up to the Mt Batur sunrise viewpoint — no trekking required unless you want to. Watch the first light over Lake Batur with breakfast on the rim. On the way back, visit Tirta Empul, the sacred water temple where you can join the purification ritual if you feel called to. Afternoon is yours — most guests nap.

Mt Batur jeep tour Sunrise breakfast Tirta Empul ritual
06

Day 6 · Ubud Culture

Monkey Forest → art market → spa → farewell dinner

A slower day. Morning walk through Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary (keep your snacks zipped). Browse the Ubud Art Market for handmade silver, sarongs and rattan bags — your host helps you bargain. Afternoon Balinese spa with flower-bath finish. Then: farewell dinner at a cliffside restaurant with group reel premiere on the big screen.

Monkey Forest Ubud Art Market Balinese spa Farewell dinner
07

Day 7 · Departure

Final breakfast & airport transfer

One last pool breakfast, final group photo, and check out. Airport transfer based on your flight time (we split into 2-3 vans). You leave with edited reels already in your gallery, a new WhatsApp group of friends, and a strong suspicion you'll be back.

FAQs

Before you book

Still on the fence? These are the questions we hear most often.

What is included in the Bali 7-day group trip?

The ₹49,999 package covers 6 nights' twin-sharing accommodation (4 nights at Adhi Jaya Kuta + 2 nights at Sens Hotel Ubud), daily breakfast and 2 Balinese dinners, all transport in private AC vans, Nusa Penida boat transfer and 4x4 island tour with lunch, all temple entry fees, Mt Batur sunrise jeep tour, an English-speaking local guide, an Indian influencer host, and group content creation. Flights, visa, lunches and personal expenses are extra.

Do I need a visa for this Bali group trip?

Indian passport holders get Visa on Arrival (VoA) at Denpasar airport for IDR 500,000 (~₹2,800) per person, valid 30 days. You can also apply for e-VoA online at evisa.imigrasi.go.id before travel. Passport must be valid 6+ months from arrival. We'll share a step-by-step visa guide after you book. Our team helps you at the arrival counter too.

Is the Bali group trip available with flights?

Yes. The "With Flights" package (₹98,000 twin-sharing) includes return economy flights from Mumbai, Delhi or Bengaluru to Denpasar via IndiGo, AirAsia or Air India. Flights are booked 6-8 weeks before departure in economy class with 1-bag baggage. Upgrades to business class or extra baggage are available at cost. Choose the "Without Flights" option if you want to book your own tickets.

Who hosts the Bali group trips?

The June 4-10, 2026 departure is hosted by NamasteeWanderrlust co-founder Parull Chaudhry and lifestyle creator Maera Mishra. The July 4-10, 2026 departure is hosted by Nyrraa M Banerji, Aditi Sharma and Nikki Sharma. Our creators personally lead welcome dinners, group activities and reel content — making your trip feel like travelling with friends.

How strenuous is the Nusa Penida day tour and Mt Batur trek?

Nusa Penida day is long (10-12 hours) but not physically demanding if you skip the optional Kelingking Beach trek-down (steep stairs, 45 minutes each way). Mt Batur is an early-morning jeep tour to a sunrise viewpoint — no trekking required. Fit travellers can choose to add the 2-hour summit hike for ₹2,500 extra. Both activities are comfortable for first-timers.

What's the cancellation policy?

60+ days before departure: 90% refund. 30-60 days: 60%. 15-30 days: 30%. Less than 15 days: no refund. Flight-inclusive cancellations are subject to airline policies and may have lower refunds. We strongly recommend purchasing travel insurance. Date swaps between June and July departures are allowed if seats are available.
